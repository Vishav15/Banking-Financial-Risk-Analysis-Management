# Banking-Financial-Risk-Analysis-Management
# OverView
# Introduction

This project demonstrates the application of Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) in a real-world risk analytics scenario within the banking and financial services sector. The goal is to analyse customer loan application data to identify patterns that distinguish reliable borrowers from potential defaulters.

# Objectives

# Identify key patterns and indicators of loan defaults.

Understand driver variables influencing repayment capability.

# Support business actions such as:

Denying risky loans.

Reducing approved loan amounts.

Offering loans at higher interest rates to riskier applicants.

By understanding these patterns, financial institutions can reduce risk exposure, improve portfolio quality, and make informed lending decisions.

# Business Understanding

When a loan application is received, the bank must decide whether to approve or reject it. This decision involves two key risks:

Business Loss Risk – If a creditworthy customer is rejected, the company loses potential business.

Financial Loss Risk – If a high-risk (likely defaulter) customer is approved, the company may suffer financial losses.

Thus, the company must balance profitability and risk minimisation.

# Loan Application Scenarios

Approved – Loan granted.

Cancelled – Client withdrew the application.

Refused – Loan rejected due to high risk or not meeting requirements.

Unused Offer – Loan cancelled at different processing stages.

# Conclusion

Through this case study, we explored how EDA can be applied to risk analytics in banking. The analysis revealed critical variables that drive loan default risk, such as income type, previous repayment behaviour, and loan history.

By leveraging these insights, financial institutions can:

Reduce default risk by identifying high-risk applicants.

Optimise profitability by approving loans for low-risk customers.

Improve decision-making by offering tailored loan terms based on risk profiles.

In conclusion, effective risk analytics and EDA help bridge the gap between financial growth and risk minimisation, enabling smarter lending strategies in the banking sector.
